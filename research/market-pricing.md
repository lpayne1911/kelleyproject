# Driveway Advocate — Market & Pricing Briefing

**Prepared:** 2026-06-20 · **Audience:** founding team · **Status:** research draft

> ℹ️ **Informational, directional research — not investment advice or a guarantee.** Market-size
> figures vary widely by methodology; many competitor prices were read from search snapshots
> because vendor pages blocked automated fetching (HTTP 403); several demand statistics trace to
> older studies. Treat dollar figures as **directional** and re-verify the flagged ones against
> primary sources before using them in a pitch deck or pricing decision.

---

## Bottom line

- **Big, dollarized pain, large TAM.** ~**53M** vehicle sales/yr (~16M new + ~20M used-retail);
  ~**80%** of new buyers finance. Per-deal pain is concrete: **$1,500–$2,400** dealer F&I profit/vehicle
  + **~$647** median financing markup (90th pct **$1,655**) + add-on markups in the hundreds-to-thousands.
- **Sentiment strongly favors a buyer's advocate:** **87%** dislike the dealership process; **61%** feel
  taken advantage of. Affordability stress is at a peak (record **~20%** of new buyers at **$1,000+/mo**;
  **$1.69T** auto debt).
- **The advisory "review-my-quote" lane is comparatively thin.** Most competitors are pricier
  **full brokers that source the car** ($600–$999+); the low end is being commoditized by **AI negotiation**
  (CarEdge ~$50) and free apps (CoPilot).
- **Pricing checks out.** Deal Rescue maps onto CarEdge's $999; the credit tier sits in the incumbent band;
  Credit-to-Keys is premium-but-defensible **as a bundle**.
- **The real white space is the bundle.** No clear competitor offers consumer-paid, buyer-side
  **credit prep + car-deal protection** together — the strongest candidate for a defensible wedge,
  but treat "first mover" as a hypothesis to validate, and note the standalone moat is thin.

> **Read:** The auto market's center of gravity is **full brokering** ($600–$999+). Driveway Advocate's
> **advisory** lane (review the buyer's own quote, flag junk fees, coach) is less crowded — but pressured
> from below by CarEdge's ~$50 AI and free apps. On credit, incumbents cluster at **$69–$150/mo + setup**;
> Dovly's freemium AI is the disruptor; the Lexington Law collapse vacated the top brand.

## 3. Substitutes & the trust wedge

The "free" car-buying programs are **dealer-funded**, not buyer fiduciaries — the structural opening:

- **Costco Auto, AAA, Sam's Club, and credit-union car-buying** services are matching/lead-gen to a dealer
  network — **AAA/Sam's/most CUs are literally "powered by TrueCar,"** and the dealer (or TrueCar referral)
  pays ([Costco](https://www.kiplinger.com/personal-finance/family-savings/costco-auto-program-how-it-works),
  [AAA/TrueCar](https://acg.truecar.com/), [Navy Federal](https://www.navyfederal.org/loans-cards/auto-loans/auto-resources/car-buying-service.html)).
- **Pricing tools (TrueCar/Edmunds/KBB) stop at the showroom door** — they set a front-end target price but
  leave the buyer alone in the **F&I office**, the highest-margin, highest-harm moment (rate markup, VSC/GAP
  with 30–60% dealer commission) ([CFPB on F&I](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-finance-and-insurance-fi-department-en-747/)).

> **Wedge:** "We work only for you, paid only by you, and we guard the **F&I office** that the free tools
> ignore." That's a credible, differentiated trust message the substitutes structurally cannot make.

## 4. Pricing — per-tier verdict vs. market

| Tier | Price | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Deal Check** (quote audit) | $99–$199 | **Competitive; possibly underpriced** as a tripwire. No clean comparable (rivals sell full-service); well below the $600+ broker band. |
| **Deal Rescue** (teardown + scripts) | $349–$999 | **Well-positioned.** Top matches **CarEdge $999**; $349 entry undercuts DMV ($600), Car Concierge Plus ($650–$750), Authority (~$945). Easy ROI vs. $2k–$3.7k claimed savings. |
| **Credit Program** | $149 + $99/mo | **Competitive, slightly below market** ($99/mo < Lexington $139.95, ≤ Credit Saint mid-tier; $149 setup in the $50–$195 norm). |
| **Credit-to-Keys** | $1,799–$3,500 | **Premium — value-stack it.** Reasonable **as a bundle** (credit ~$99–$140/mo × months + ~$999 concierge + sourcing); $1,799 lands, **$3,500 only on high-WTP / credit-challenged** segments. Anchor on combined savings + approval outcomes. |
| Fleet/Family | Custom | No public benchmark; price on value/volume. |

WTP context: 68% of affluent investors will pay for financial advice; flat-fee planning ~$250/hr or
~$265/mo ([Cerulli](https://www.thinkadvisor.com/2024/09/30/affluent-investors-are-increasingly-willing-to-pay-for-advice-cerulli/),
[Kitces](https://www.kitces.com/blog/financial-advisors-charge-services-fee-structure-advisory-firm-profession-aum-pricing-insight/)).

## 5. Differentiation & moat

- **White space = the bundle.** No clear competitor offers consumer-paid, buyer-side **credit prep + car-deal
  protection** together; "credit-to-keys" searches surface only *dealer-side* subprime lead-gen and
  buy-here-pay-here ([example](https://www.keysandcredit.com/about-us/)). This is the strongest defensible
  angle — **but validate it; it's absence-of-evidence, not proof.**
- **Moat is thin on the auto side alone.** Low entry barriers, costly trust-building, human-review
  scalability limits, and **encroaching AI negotiation** (CarEdge AI claims ~$1,500 avg savings) all erode a
  pure "human advocate" edge ([CarEdge AI](https://caredge.com/guides/ai-is-poised-to-change-how-we-buy-cars)).
- **Implications:** (a) lead with the **bundle + fiduciary trust** narrative the dealer-funded substitutes
  can't match; (b) use the credit engine for **recurring revenue + a pre-sold auto client** (the deck's
  flywheel — supported here); (c) build **AI-assisted review** into the ops line early so you're not
  out-commoditized on the audit; (d) the credit half's compliance rigor (see `compliance.md`) is itself a
  trust moat vs. the post-Lexington-Law credibility vacuum.

## 6. Recommended next validation steps
1. **Smoke-test demand** for the advisory tiers (landing page + paid "upload your quote" → Deal Check at
   $99–$149) before scaling human review.
2. **Confirm the "no one bundles credit-to-keys" hypothesis** with a focused competitor scan.
3. **Re-verify the flagged stats** (financing-markup cluster, CR extended-warranty, 2016 sentiment survey,
   CarEdge/Authority live pricing) against primary sources before any external deck.
4. **Pressure-test Credit-to-Keys at $1,799 vs. $3,500** with real prospects; the bundle math supports
   ~$1,799 broadly, $3,500 only for high-WTP segments.
