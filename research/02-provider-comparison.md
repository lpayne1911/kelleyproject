# Provider Comparison

All ratings/positioning below are **consumer-reported / public** as of mid-2026 and should
be re-verified before publication. Price ranges are **estimated** order-of-magnitude bands
for comparison, not quotes. See [`sources.md`](sources.md).

## C. Dealer-sold administrators (the "back office" behind F&I plans)

These rarely face the consumer directly but are the obligor on many dealer-sold contracts:
**Fidelity Warranty Services**, **Zurich**, **CNA National**, **Dealers Assurance
Company**, **JM&A Group**, **Portfolio**, **SAFE-Guard**. Quality is generally high
(backed by AM Best–rated insurers); the markup applied at the F&I desk is the variable.

## D. Credit-union / membership channel

Many credit unions resell mechanical breakdown coverage (often via **Route 66**, **CURO**,
or administrator partners) at **low markup** as a member benefit. This is frequently the
**cheapest legitimate price** and a powerful reference point for "fair price" — Driveway
Advocate should capture local CU pricing where available.

## Classification quick-reference

- **Administers own claims:** Endurance, olive, Everything Breaks, OEMs (via dealer
  network), most dealer-sold administrators.
- **Broker (resells administrators):** CarShield, CARCHEX, autopom!.
- **AM Best matters most** for the **insurer/backer** behind the obligor — always verify
  the CLIP carrier's rating, not just the marketing brand
  ([AM Best](https://web.ambest.com/ratings-services/bests-credit-ratings)).
